Wildlife Safety Tips for Uganda Safaris

Exploring Uganda’s national parks is an incredible experience, but safety is key. Whether you’re on a savanna safari or a gorilla trekking adventure, these tips will help you stay safe.

1. Listen to Your Guide

Always follow your guide’s instructions—they’re experts in navigating the wilderness and ensuring your safety around wildlife.

2. Maintain a Safe Distance

Keep a respectful distance from animals, especially during gorilla trekking (at least 7 meters) or game drives. Never attempt to feed or touch wildlife.

3. Stay in the Vehicle During Game Drives

Unless your guide says it’s safe, remain in the vehicle during game drives to avoid unexpected encounters with predators.

4. Wear Appropriate Clothing

Long sleeves, pants, and closed shoes protect you from insects and thorny vegetation, especially in dense forests.

5. Be Aware of Your Surroundings

Stay alert for signs of wildlife activity, like rustling bushes or animal tracks, and avoid wandering off on your own.
